Yesterday, I had my local interview where they wanted to learn what I did in the service and during the interval. Long story short, they’re going to try to get me in as a drone operator. I’ve said it before, but I should have prepared better for this. If I had a drone pilots certificate, it’d be a slam dunk. The instructors are seeing what they can do for me. The Legion really doesn’t want to train people, they want to take people with preexisting skills and slot them in. Anyway, wish me luck.

Fire watch that night was interesting. I was filling in for some quitters on another team, so I had to go to a different floor than where I sleep. When my shift started everyone was supposed to be asleep, but when I arrived the entire group was not only awake, but they were all on their feet in the common area. Two instructors were there as well. I couldn’t really understand what anyone was saying, but they were clearly angry about something. I thought there was going to be a brawl. The cadre calmed them down eventually, and I came to understand they were pissed about not getting paid to be here. (Salary starts at the end of this stage, after you get a contract.) Apparently, none of these Colombians understood this until moments prior to my arrival. I don’t know why two of them wanted to fight each other about this, but we had to break them up by force. I was pretty wired after that and didn’t get much sleep that night.

The next day we received some intelligence that Russia was planning a massive drone, airstrike, cruise missile, ballistic missile attack with targets not far from us. We kept our bags zipped and prepared to rush to the bomb shelter at any time last night. What little sleep I got was full of the strangest dreams.

The rest of the englishers showed up today, even a few Americans. More surprisingly, one was from my home state. My roomie and I had to move down a level to be with them, which is good, but the toilet in this unit doesn’t work, which is bad. ;-) Some people have to brush their teeth with the shower, so it’s five stars all around. I am officially not the group leader anymore, so that’s a load off. Got to go shopping again today and picked up a local SIM card, which they sell behind the counter at the market here like you might purchase stamps.

Tomorrow is interviews with the unit. At lunch I learned what squadrons to avoid and which to ask for, absolutely invaluable information. I got to play with a DJI quadcopter today. It seems simple enough. I’ve only been playing with dual stick controllers for 30 some years now, so… again, wish me luck.
